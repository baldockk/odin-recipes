Spaghetti Bolognaise

Description

Spaghetti Bolognese is a beloved Italian classic, featuring a rich, savory meat sauce served over perfectly cooked spaghetti. The sauce, known as "ragù alla Bolognese," is made by simmering ground beef or pork with onions, garlic, tomatoes, and a blend of aromatic herbs and spices. This slow-cooked mixture develops deep flavors that coat every strand of pasta, creating a satisfying and comforting dish that's perfect for any occasion.

Whether enjoyed as a family dinner or served to guests, Spaghetti Bolognese is a timeless favorite. With its hearty meat sauce, tender spaghetti, and a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese on top, it’s a delicious, filling meal that brings people together with every bite.

Steps

  1. Prepare the sauce: In a large pan, sauté chopped onion, garlic, carrot, and celery in olive oil until softened. Add ground meat and cook until browned. Stir in canned tomatoes (or puree), red wine (if using), herbs, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 30-45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is thickened and flavorful.
  2. Cook the pasta: While the sauce is simmering, cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions. Drain the pasta, then toss it with the Bolognese sauce. Serve with a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese on top.
